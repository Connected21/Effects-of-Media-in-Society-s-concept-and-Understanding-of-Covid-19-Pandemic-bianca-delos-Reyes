The role of social media during a Pandemic

Media has always been a vital part of the society, it can mean life and death to so many specially in this trying times. Covid-19 has become a pandemic and global medical issue up until this moment. It has the possibility of inflicting all humanity if not addressed and suppressed properly.

Access to media has been very necessary in the midst of this pandemic as it somehow save lives from the threat of massive virus infliction. Additionally, Media has been a useful tool in disseminating useful and essential information about any news, entertainment or in this case, any updates about COVID-19.

It serves as the channel of people to keep updated and mindful. Specifically speaking, mass media impacts the people’s awareness and preparedness for it is responsible for the deliverance of safety precautions, tips, quarantine policies, quarantine facilities, mortality rate, death rate and even the updates of aiming at curve-flattening.

Media facilitates change among the members of the nation as all media personnel are expected to spearhead and front-lined this medical crisis alongside with medical experts and public service centered people. Hunger for information, news and public services are served by the media jointly with the government. Media are expected to disperse and broadcast more updates Especially now that the "new normal" is fast approaching; where everyone in today’s time are required to wear face masks and observe social distancing.

Additionally, the implementation of community quarantines around the country to restrict people from going out and reminding them to always stay indoors for their safety and protection. Innovations in human interaction are also encouraged parallel with the advent of technology and platforms discoveries such as zoom meetings which allows office employees to continue their work, students to study and even event celebrations, business meetings and worshipping practices to still be continued at home. Moreover, media not only has it eased everyone into ignorance during the world wide quarantine , it also had opened a lot of opportunities to have a wide and broader discussion about politics and the like. Where it has been a site where Filipinos are speaking up for their rights to be heard, to let every unaware citizens hear their thoughts about the discomposed and unorganized government and their plan of action for covid-19 full elimination.

Media particularly social media made everybody enlightened of everything that’s happening in the midst of this pandemic. However, it is doubtlessly a truth that misleading information and misinformation alarms people so much to the extent that it causes so much stress and anxiety to them.

Disinformation and misinformation are worsening the situation instead of prompting to clampdown covid-19 it causes panic and apprehension among people. Recently, panic and excessive buying has been reported and various protocols were issued to restrict unrestrained shopping of basic grocery items such as canned goods, bread, coffee etc. Now that everyone almost can gain media particulars, discernment of what’s real and unreal is crucial.

A SOURCE OF MISINFORMATION

Disinformation, misinformation widespread fake news are all forms of downright lying and distortion of truths are very rampant nowadays as it can easily be circulated through words of mouth which resulted to the failure of stretching and empowering the goal of strengthening spreading truths and facts. Circulation of fabricated information in media has an intensifier effect. It resulted to drastic effects upon people.

The rate of people experiencing mental instability, mental illness and mental health issues escalated. Anxious people became more anxious because of information overload, information fabrication, and information exaggeration.

Meanwhile, media industry was given much more pressure to deliver credible, reliable and full of certainty informations and news as pervasive transmission of fake news became rampat. Everybody are sources of information and have the capacity to incorporate themselves in issues like this but it is important to always consider relaying true and fair information and news!

What you should know about COVID-19 to protect yourself and others

Know about COVID-19

Coronavirus (COVID-19) is an illness caused by a virus that can spread from personto person. The virus that causes COVID-19 is a new coronavirus that has spread throughoutthe world. COVID-19 symptoms can range from mild(or no symptoms) to severe illness.

World Health Organization

Against Covid-19 Pandemic

Practice social distancing

Buy groceries and medicine, go to the doctor, and complete banking activities online when possible. If you must go in person, stay at least 6 feet away from others and disinfect items you must touch. Get deliveries and takeout, and limit in-person contact as much as possible.

World Health Organization

Against Covid-19 Pandemic

Know how COVID-19 is spread

You can become infected by coming into close contact (about 6 feet or twoarm lengths) with a person who has COVID-19. COVID-19 is primarily spreadfrom person to person. You can become infected from respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. You may also be able to get it by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it, and then by touching your mouth, nose, or eyes.

World Health Organization

Against Covid-19 Pandemic

Prevent the spread of COVID-19

Stay home if you are sick, except to get medical care. Avoid public transportation, ride-sharing, or taxis. Separate yourself from other people and pets in your home. There is no specific treatment for COVID-19, but you can seek medical care to help relieve your symptoms. If you need medical attention, call ahead.

World Health Organization

Against Covid-19 Pandemic

Protect yourself and others

There is currently no vaccine to protect against COVID-19. The best way to protect yourself is to avoid being exposed to the virus that causes COVID-19. Stay home as much as possible and avoid close contact with others. Wear a cloth face covering that covers your nose and mouth in public settings. Clean and disinfect frequentlytouched surfaces. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.

World Health Organization

Against Covid-19 Pandemic

Know your risk forsevere illness

Everyone is at risk ofgetting COVID-19. Older adults and people of any age who have serious underlying medical conditions may be at higher risk for more severe illness.
